<p><p>The High Purity Synthetic Quartz Glass market is primarily segmented into two types based on manufacturing processes: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Plasma Deposition. CVD involves a chemical reaction to produce high-purity quartz via gas phase reactants, allowing for precise control over material properties. In contrast, Plasma Deposition utilizes plasma-induced energy to deposit quartz films on substrates. Both methods are crucial for applications in electronics, optics, and telecommunications, offering distinct advantages in purity, structural integrity, and customization.</p></p>
