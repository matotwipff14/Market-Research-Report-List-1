<p><p>The organic hydroponic substrate market includes key types like coir, peat moss, and others. Coir, derived from coconut husks, provides excellent moisture retention and aeration, making it popular for sustainable growing. Peat moss, harvested from sphagnum moss, offers high water retention and is often used for seed starting and root development. Other substrates may include perlite, vermiculite, and rice husk, catering to diverse cultivation needs. Each substrate type supports optimal plant growth in hydroponic systems with distinct characteristics.</p></p>
