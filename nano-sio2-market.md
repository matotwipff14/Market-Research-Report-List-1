<p><p>The Nano-SiO2 market can be categorized into two primary production methods: the dry method and the wet method. The dry method involves the mechanical milling of silica materials, resulting in nanoparticles with limited control over size and distribution. In contrast, the wet method utilizes chemical processes, such as sol-gel techniques, to achieve precise control over particle size, morphology, and surface properties. Both methods cater to diverse applications, including cosmetics, electronics, and construction, depending on the required characteristics of the nanoparticles.</p></p>
