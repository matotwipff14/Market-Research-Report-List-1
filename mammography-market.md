<p><p>The mammography market comprises several types of imaging technologies. Analog mammography uses traditional film to capture images, while digital mammography offers enhanced image quality and easier storage. Computed radiography employs imaging plates to convert X-rays into digital images, facilitating better workflow. Digital radiography uses flat-panel detectors for instant image acquisition and processing, improving efficiency. Breast tomosynthesis, or 3D mammography, creates detailed images of breast tissue, allowing for better detection of abnormalities and reducing false positives, thus enhancing diagnostic accuracy.</p></p>
