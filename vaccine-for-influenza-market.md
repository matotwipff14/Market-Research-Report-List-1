<p><p>The influenza vaccine market primarily comprises trivalent and quadrivalent vaccines. Trivalent influenza vaccines are designed to protect against three selected strains: two A strains and one B strain. Conversely, quadrivalent vaccines target four strains, adding an additional B strain for broader protection. The quadrivalent vaccines have gained popularity due to their enhanced ability to guard against circulating virus strains, thereby improving overall effectiveness and reducing the risk of influenza outbreaks. Both types are crucial for seasonal flu prevention.</p></p>
