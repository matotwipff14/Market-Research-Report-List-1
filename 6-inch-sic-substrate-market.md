Conductive substrates facilitate efficient current flow, making them suitable for high-power applications, including power electronics and electric vehicles. In contrast, semi-insulated substrates provide a degree of electrical insulation, ideal for RF devices and high-frequency applications, where interference reduction is crucial. Each type serves distinct markets, addressing the diverse needs for thermal management, efficiency, and reliability in advanced electronic systems.</p></p>
