<p><p>Insulin pumps are external devices that deliver insulin to manage diabetes effectively. The market comprises two primary types: tethered pumps and disposable/patch pumps. Tethered pumps are connected to the body via a catheter, allowing continuous insulin delivery while offering enhanced control. In contrast, disposable or patch pumps are smaller, adhesive devices that provide a more discreet and portable option, delivering insulin without tubes. Both types aim to improve patient adherence and optimize glucose control for better health outcomes.</p></p>
