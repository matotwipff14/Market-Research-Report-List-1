<p><p>Serum Free Freezing Media is designed for cryopreservation of cells without the use of animal-derived serum. The market is divided into two primary types: DMSO-based and DMSO-free formulations. DMSO (dimethyl sulfoxide) is commonly used for its cryoprotective properties, preventing ice crystal formation during freezing. Conversely, DMSO-free media cater to researchers seeking alternatives for sensitive cell types, reducing toxicity and enhancing viability post-thaw. Both formulations play critical roles in advancing cell-based research and therapeutic applications.</p></p>
